What is Beersbee?

BEER + FRISBEE = FUN!

Beersbee is a yard game involving (2) two teams of (2) two players each attempting to knock an object (empty bottle) off of the opposing team's pole using a frisbee.

Each team requires one pole and one object (bottle). The poles should be stationed 27 feet (8.2m) apart with an object on top of each. The teams take turns throwing the frisbee at the object attempting to strike either the Beersbee pole or the object in hopes of knocking the object to the ground. The defending team attempts to catch both the frisbee and the bottle.

These are the basic rules of the game

  • The "referree" will flip a coin to decide which team starts
  • Each team will be comprised of two players. Teams will alternate throwing at the other side, and players will alternate throwing for each team
  • Every player must be holding a beverage of their choice in one hand at all times during play, leaving only one hand to play the game with
  • Some throws will be deemed uncatchable, resulting in 0 points for either team. This counts as the throwing teams attempt, and play continues
  • Players may not break the plane of the pole, and/or interfere with the frisbee at any time before it crosses the poles plane
  • Players must stay behind the pole. Any interference results in the loss of the next throw by the team that interferes

Play continues until one of the teams has 21 total points. You must win by 2 points, so the maximum score may go beyond 21 points

  • OFFENSE is awarded 1 point if the frisbee is not caught by the defending team
  • OFFENSE is awarded 2 points if the object is knocked to the ground and not caught by the defensive team
  • OFFENSE is awarded a maximum of 3 points if the object is knocked to the ground and the disk is not caught by the defensive team
  • DEFENSE is awarded 1 point if the object is knocked off the pole and caught inside (bottom) the caught frisbee
  • DEFENSE is awarded 2 point if the object is knocked off the pole and caught outside (top) the caught frisbee
